Tattoo and Permanent Makeup Aftercare

Tattoo / Permanent Makeup Prep

  • Hydrate!

    We want you to always be hydrated because it’s good for your overall health and your skin, but especially for your tattooing or permanent make up appointment!

  • Exfoliate and moisturize!

    We can always benefit from removing impurities from our skin and moisturizing but this is especially helpful leading up to your appointment. Please do not moisturize or exfoliate immediately before your appointment as it can irritate the skin or cause complications with the tattoo machine.

  • Rest!

    We know it’s hard to sleep when your so excited for your new tattoo or permanent make up service! Try to get a good nights sleep prior to your appointment so your body can heal properly.

  • Refrain from drugs & alcohol!

    Drugs and alcohol can inhibit the bodies natural healing process, cause complications during your appointment, and effect the quality of your tattoo.

  • Eat a snack!

    Please make sure you arrive at your appointment with a full belly! Getting tattooed uses a lot of the bodies energy, and can cause fatigue or may even make you pass out on an empty stomach. Did you know? your body actually is able to handle pain better when you aren't hungry!

  • Waiver and ID!

    Please fill out any waivers that have been sent to you prior to the appointment to speed up your intake process, and also please bring a valid ID card or Driver’s license.


Permanent Makeup FAQs

  • What is permanent make up?

    Permanent makeup refers to cosmetic tattoos that help you recreate your favorite makeup looks in a semi-permanent.

  • Do I have to shave my eyebrows off?

    NOOOOOO! The coolest thing about cosmetic tattooing on the brows is that it naturally blends your natural brow hairs in with the machine strokes!

  • When do I need it touched up?

    Typically results last about 2-3 years before needing touched up.

  • Does it hurt?

    It may be uncomfortable, but we use a topical anesthetic to numb the area and reduce discomfort.

  • How do I know how mine will look?

    Each permanent make up appointment begins with a through consultation to discuss the shape, size, color, and style with each client.
